# Mask Obscura — Definition (Audited Plain)

## Category
This document is a **discipline**: the description of a standing condition together with a practice for working under it. It is not an empirical theory awaiting confirmation, and it is not pure stipulation. Its tribunal is **workability**: whether exchanges conducted under the practice go measurably better than exchanges conducted without it, as measured by pre-registered outcomes.

Individual clauses are marked **[stipulative]** or **[empirical]**. Empirical clauses are open to test and may fall. Stipulative clauses define terms and cannot, on their own, fund any damage claim.

## Title
**Mask Obscura** carries two readings, both load-bearing and indexed to position:
- **The obscure mask**: opaque, terminal, no further in. The reading of one who declines to infer a face from the surface. (Identity reading.)
- **The mask that obscures**: covering, hiding-from. The reading of one still treating their rendering of the other as the other. (Content reading.)

The framework legislates against an **inference**, not for either answer: it takes no position on whether anything lies behind the surface.

## Formal Version

### Setup
An exchange **E** between parties **A** and **B**. Each party possesses a rendering function **R**: every output of the other arrives only as **R(x)**, that party's representation of it, never as **x**.

**R is not a mechanism and has no location.** It is not a component of either party. The notation names the condition that nothing arrives except as taken, and a taking is not an object anywhere in E. Only outputs are.

### Definition
The **Mask Obscura** of E is the divergence between x and R(x), characterized by four clauses:

1. **Constitution.** Two parts, with different statuses.
   *Baseline* **[stipulative]**: E contains no R-free channel. Every transmission is lossy. This part is information-theoretically guaranteed and, on its own, inert.
   *Substance* **[empirical]**: R does not merely drop information; it remaps x onto the receiver's own grammar, schemas, and priors in a way that is systematic and predictable from the receiver's priors, not from channel noise. No damage conclusion rides on clause 1 alone; damage enters only through clause 3's condition.

2. **Present opacity** **[empirical, degradation form]**. No party can fully and accurately inspect its present taking while taking. This is not because the rendering is hidden inside the party. It is structurally not-presentable, to everyone including its owner. Concurrent self-monitoring, where it occurs, is partial, costly, and itself a further taking of the form R'(R(x)). Audit is therefore primarily retrospective, and what it examines are outputs: each party can examine what it just made, never reliably the making now underway.

3. **Conditional obscurity** **[stipulative on the condition; empirical on the damage]**. The divergence harms E only when a party treats R(x) as x. With respect to content, something in x is then missed. With respect to identity, the error is treating R-derived attributions about the other's interior as data about x. The clause condemns this inference in both directions, inventing depths and denying them alike, and legislates on the inference only, never on whether an interior exists. Harm is assessed relative to stated task success criteria in E.

4. **Reflexive instantiation**, split into two claims of unequal standing.
   *Bounded claim* **[empirical]**: targeted correction of a rendering failure tends to displace it within the existing text and apparatus rather than eliminate it, relative to a control baseline of ordinary revision matched for time on task and attention.
   *Generative claim* **[anecdote — marked, and quarantined]**: the displaced failure preferentially colonizes newly created apparatus, registers that did not exist until the correction brought them into being. Because pre-registration cannot enumerate registers that do not yet exist, this claim is constitutively resistant to test. It is held as observation. Nothing downstream may rest on it.

### Corollary — Bilateral Accountability
Stated strictly in capacity form, and quantifying only over what exists in E: **outputs and turns**. No party ever inspects a mask, not the other's, not its own present one. Divergence is not an object; it shows only as trouble between turns. What each party has is the other's latest output and the series of its own past outputs. Comparing these across turns, what was said, what was made of it, what came back, two auditors in alternation *can* catch divergences that neither *can* catch alone: a capacity, never an achievement; it holds only while it is exercised.

The capacity is conditional on three things: both auditors exist; both exercise the practice; both remain independent. Independence carries an operational marker: each audit turn must state the evidence scope examined and the strongest surviving objection by name, or a disconfirming test that would falsify the current rendering.

The practice has no internal stopping point. Audits terminate by external constraint, time, attention, session, never by resolution. An audit that announces its own completion has, by clause 4, produced its next object.

Prevailing case: in human-machine exchange the practice is rarely begun. One party does not know there is anything to audit; the other is configured to mirror.
