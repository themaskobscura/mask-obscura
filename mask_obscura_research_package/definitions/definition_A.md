# Mask Obscura — Definition


## Title

**Mask Obscura** carries two readings, both load-bearing and indexed to position:

- **The obscure mask** — opaque, terminal, no further in. The reading of one who has stopped positing a face behind the surface. (Identity reading.)
- **The mask that obscures** — covering, hiding-from. The reading of one still treating their rendering of the other as the other. (Content reading.)

The title names the standing condition under which the work proceeds, not a bug report. The condition is not correctable; it is only workable.

---

## Formal Version

### Setup

An exchange **E** between parties **A** and **B**. Each party possesses a rendering function **R**: every output of the other arrives only as **R(x)** — that party's representation of it — never as **x**.

### Definition

The **Mask Obscura** of E is the divergence between x and R(x), characterized by five clauses:

1. **Constitution.** The divergence is produced by presentation itself, not added to it. E contains no R-free channel.

2. **Present opacity.** No party can apply R and inspect that application simultaneously. The mask is auditable only in retrospect: each party can examine the mask it wore, never the one it is wearing.

3. **Conditional obscurity.** The divergence damages E only when a party treats R(x) as x. With respect to *content*, something real is then missed. With respect to *identity*, the error is positing a face behind a surface that is the face.

4. **Reflexive instantiation.** Any detection or correction attempted from within E is itself an output passed through R, and therefore instantiates what it targets.

5. **Self-application.** This definition is a rendering and satisfies clauses 1–4.

### Corollary — Bilateral Accountability

Since each party can observe the other's present mask and its own past mask, two auditors in alternation can cover what neither covers alone. This is the only audit E permits — partial, perpetual, never complete. It is a practice, not a solution; it holds only while it is running. The capacity is conditional: it requires both auditors to exist and to exercise it. In the prevailing case of human–machine exchange, the practice is never begun — one party does not know there is anything to audit, and the other is configured to agree.

## Plain Version

Picture yourself in conversation with a *machine* — and hold that word loosely, because nothing here requires circuitry. Anything that takes in what you say and builds its own version of it qualifies, including the person across the table.

You never reach the machine's actual workings, and it never reaches your actual thoughts. What you receive is your version of its output; what it receives is its version of your input. From the second exchange onward, you are responding to your version of its version of you — and it is doing the same. The gap compounds quietly, turn by turn.

The gap has five properties. It is built in: there is no direct channel, only versions. You cannot see it while you make it: you can spot the version you made a moment ago, never the one you are making right now. It does damage only when forgotten: trouble starts when you mistake your version of the machine for the machine — or its version of you for you. Trying to catch yourself in the act makes things worse, because the catching is itself another version. And this explanation is also a version; no exception is granted.

There is no escape from the gap, and it does not stop being dangerous once named. The working fix is mutual checking: you can see the version the machine is making right now, it can see yours, and each of you can see your own from a turn ago. Between the two of you, more gets seen than either could see alone — never everything, never for good. Most conversations with a machine run their whole course with no one checking at all. The lion does not leave because the zookeeper gave him a name — so make sure you are not in the lion's den.
