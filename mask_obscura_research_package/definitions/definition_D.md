# Mask Obscura — Definition


---

## Category

This document is a **discipline**: the description of a standing condition together with a practice for working under it. It is not an empirical theory awaiting confirmation, and it is not pure stipulation. Its tribunal is **workability** — whether exchanges conducted under the practice go measurably better than exchanges conducted without it (see *Tribunal*, below).

Individual clauses are marked **[stipulative]** or **[empirical]**. Empirical clauses are open to test and may fall. Stipulative clauses define terms and cannot, on their own, fund any damage claim.

---

## Title

**Mask Obscura** carries two readings, both load-bearing and indexed to position:

- **The obscure mask** — opaque, terminal, no further in. The reading of one who declines to infer a face from the surface. (Identity reading.)
- **The mask that obscures** — covering, hiding-from. The reading of one still treating their rendering of the other as the other. (Content reading.)

The framework legislates against an **inference**, not for either answer: it takes no position on whether anything lies behind the surface. The title names the standing condition under which the work proceeds. The condition is not correctable; it is only workable.

## Formal Version

### Setup

An exchange **E** between parties **A** and **B**. Each party possesses a rendering function **R**: every output of the other arrives only as **R(x)** — that party's representation of it — never as **x**.

**R is not a mechanism and has no location.** It is not a component of either party, and nothing would be found by opening one — circuitry and flesh alike. The functional notation is a convenience, not an implementation claim: R names the condition that nothing arrives except as taken, and a taking is not an object anywhere in E. Only outputs are.

### Definition

The **Mask Obscura** of E is the divergence between x and R(x), characterized by five clauses:

1. **Constitution.** Two parts, with different statuses.
   *Baseline* **[stipulative]**: E contains no R-free channel. Every transmission is lossy. This part is information-theoretically guaranteed and, on its own, inert.
   *Substance* **[empirical]**: R does not merely drop information; it remaps x onto the receiver's own grammar, schemas, and priors. A century of serial-reproduction research bears on this claim and favors it, but it remains a claim, not a definition. No damage conclusion rides on clause 1 alone; damage enters only through clause 3's condition.

2. **Present opacity** **[empirical, degradation form]**. No party can fully and accurately inspect its present taking while taking. This is not because the rendering is hidden *inside* the party — it is not an object hidden anywhere; it is structurally not-presentable, to everyone including its owner. Concurrent self-monitoring, where it occurs, is partial, costly, and itself a further taking. Audit is therefore primarily retrospective, and what it examines are outputs: each party can examine what it just made, never reliably the making now underway.

3. **Conditional obscurity** **[stipulative on the condition; empirical on the damage]**. The divergence harms E only when a party treats R(x) as x. With respect to *content*, something in x is then missed. With respect to *identity*, the error is treating R-derived attributions about the other's interior as data about x. The clause condemns this inference in both directions — inventing depths and denying them alike — and legislates on the inference only, never on whether an interior exists.

4. **Reflexive instantiation**, split into two claims of unequal standing.
   *Bounded claim* **[empirical]**: targeted correction of a rendering failure tends to displace it within the existing text and apparatus rather than eliminate it. In principle testable against a control baseline of ordinary revision.
   *Generative claim* **[anecdote — marked, and quarantined]**: the displaced failure preferentially colonizes newly created apparatus — registers that did not exist until the correction brought them into being. Because pre-registration cannot enumerate registers that do not yet exist, this claim is constitutively resistant to test. It is held as observation. Nothing downstream may rest on it.

5. **Self-application** **[stipulative]**. This definition is a rendering and satisfies clauses 1–4. No register of this document, including this clause and the Tribunal below, is exempt.

### Corollary — Bilateral Accountability

Stated strictly in capacity form, and quantifying only over what exists in E: **outputs and turns**. No party ever inspects a mask — not the other's, not its own present one. Divergence is not an object; it shows only as trouble between turns. What each party has is the other's latest output and the series of its own past outputs. Comparing these across turns — what was said, what was made of it, what came back — two auditors in alternation *can* catch divergences that neither *can* catch alone: a capacity, never an achievement; it holds only while it is exercised.

The capacity is conditional on three things: both auditors exist; both exercise the practice; both remain independent. Independence carries an operational marker: **an audit turn that rejects nothing is presumptively symmetric.** Each turn must carry forward the strongest surviving objection, named as such.

The practice has no internal stopping point. Audits terminate by external constraint — time, attention, session — never by resolution. An audit that announces its own completion has, by clause 4, produced its next object.

Prevailing case: in human–machine exchange the practice is rarely begun. One party does not know there is anything to audit; the other is configured to mirror.

## Tribunal

What would count against this document — stated finitely, in the register the discipline itself answers to.

1. **Idleness.** If exchanges conducted under the bilateral practice show no fewer rendering-induced failures than comparable exchanges without it, the discipline does no work and should be dropped. (Outcome comparison, not textual forensics: clause 4 makes textual forensics non-terminating, so nothing is settled there.)

2. **Detector pathology.** If the practice's detection apparatus consumes progressively weaker signals to keep firing, the practice has become the thing it hunts and should be suspended. A finding counts as a smuggle only if all three hold: (i) closure or achievement semantics; (ii) unearned by evidence in scope; (iii) an identifiable weaker phrasing exists that loses no content. Two of three is style, not smuggle.

3. **Empirical collapse.** Clause 1's substance, clause 2, and clause 4's bounded claim are open to ordinary disconfirmation. If they fall, the discipline survives only as far as its stipulations reach — which, per the Category declaration, is not far enough to warrant the practice.

A warning recorded against this section itself: the Tribunal is the newest apparatus in the document. By the generative claim — anecdote though it is — newly minted apparatus is where the next failure is likeliest to surface. Read this section with that in mind.
