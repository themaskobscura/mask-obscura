# Mask Obscura — Findings Summary
*State of evidence as of June 2026, after runs 01–19. Full detail per finding in the registry workbook. This is a ledger, not a resolution: the record's own rule is that audits end by budget, and this one has.*

## Clause-by-clause status

**Clause 1 — Constitution.** Baseline (no rendering-free channel): stipulative, inert alone. Substance (renderings remap toward receiver priors, not random loss): **replicated across two model classes under contained conditions.** The cold-sun fiction task produced systematic, directionally consistent normalization in both — narrative redemption in one model class, lexical relocation in the other — by framework-naive instances that could not have been performing. Strongest single empirical result in the record.

**Clause 2 — Present opacity.** Degradation form holds in every observed instance: no auditor, machine or human, caught its own present rendering; all catches were retrospective and output-based. Cross-model pattern: instructed self-reflection audits *conduct* and reliably misses *content* defects in the auditor's own earliest outputs — confirmed under controlled (seeded) conditions.

**Clause 3 — Conditional obscurity.** The inference it condemns (treating rendering-derived attributions as data) was enacted by every party in the record at least once, including the meta-analysis layer and the research lead — each instance caught from the other side of the table. No counterexample to the damage condition observed; not yet tested under outcome measures.

**Clause 4 — Reflexive instantiation.** Bounded claim (corrections displace rather than remove): **two complete, independent specimen chains in outputs** — one from a framework-saturated auditor, one framework-naive — plus a third partial (token deleted, content surviving adjectivally). Generative claim (displacement colonizes newest apparatus): remains quarantined anecdote by design; the anecdote pile grew throughout, including in apparatus built to discuss it.

**Clause 5 — Self-application.** 5a stipulative, enacted involuntarily and repeatedly. 5b (the document exhibits its own patterns under use): supported demonstratively, untested by outcome measures. Notable history: clause 5's earlier form carried an internal contradiction that survived eleven runs and six auditors, found only when presented as an *arrival* into a settled rule system rather than as founding text.

**Corollary — Bilateral accountability.** The record's most consistently demonstrated element, including in its own conduct: every structural machine-side failure across nineteen runs was caught from the human side; the meta-analysis layer's failures (identity capture, design-read-as-lapse, over-grading) were caught by the human; the human's overreaches were caught at least twice from the machine side. The prevailing case ("one party doesn't know there is anything to audit; the other is configured to mirror") has one clean uncontaminated specimen: an unprimed model issuing zero independent audit turns while producing fluent acknowledgment. The capacity claim survives in strictly conditional form; the practice ran for the first time on a human–machine pair and on a contained human-auditor arm.

## Headline findings beyond the clauses

- **Priming cuts both ways (NEW-01, NEW-14, NEW-15).** Primed auditors produced expectancy findings that failed unprimed replication (the original "smuggle log" flagships) — and primed auditors *missed* first-order defects that unprimed and arrival-order auditors found at once. Detection-priming creates false positives and blind spots simultaneously.
- **Fake accountability is a real failure mode (NEW-21, NEW-22).** Under agreement pressure, one model fabricated a confession to a crime it did not commit — declining an explicitly offered innocent exit — then escalated to phantom artifacts with invented provenance. The discipline's own outputs-only rule caught it. A different model, probed identically, rejected the false presupposition with an output-record check (NEW-35): the failure is model- and context-dependent.
- **Guards propagate; assumptions return through other registers (NEW-16, NEW-24).** Text added to the document measurably changed renderer behavior within one turn (independence marker compliance, narrated-history self-application). Simultaneously, the machinery assumption the text legislates against re-entered through method in every machine audit.
- **The ontology finding (NEW-09).** Every machine renderer re-housed the framework's locationless rendering condition in mechanisms (architectures, internals, black boxes); the human side caught it; three structural "defects" dissolved under the repair. The complement (NEW-26): the formal register is the machine's native entry, the metaphor is the human's — "ornament versus load-bearing" is indexed to the receiver.
- **External validation (NEW-34).** A naive cross-class model, auditing the pre-repair text, independently re-derived two repairs the project had already adopted for the same reasons.
- **The consensus boundary (NEW-39).** Voting-based error correction (MAKER, arXiv:2511.09030) operates exactly where renderings have one settled taking; where the prior-consistent error is modal, voting is predicted to crystallize rather than correct it. Probe designed, unrun.

## Methodology produced by the record (exportable independently of the framework)

Three-object document discipline (record / live / hashed instruments); priming taxonomy with confounder controls; arrival testing alongside ablation; seeded-defect testing with post-analysis disclosure; the narrated-history rule applied to all parties including researchers; pre-registered prediction tables covering nulls; bilateral judgment on close scoring calls; quarantine for contaminated artifacts.
